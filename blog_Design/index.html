Is the future in the past?

What happens when the old-timers retire

Just this week, I was talking to one of the old friends my father had during high-school. Interestingly enough, it turned out that he was a mainframe engineer in IBM, working primarily in an administrative function. However, the interesting thing about him wasn't what he did, but rather how he got there.

Carlos, the DBA/Maingrame virtuoso, got his start repairing critical errors in production databses, quicker and cleaner than anyone around. Bit of an understatement to say he was special. After a while working on emergency projects, he was hired by IBM as a project manager. Finally, he was in a more stable position.
